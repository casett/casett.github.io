Outreach

Mentoring

I believe that mentoring is an invaluable opportunity to help support interns experience science, as well as to help them explore possible future careers and further their education. I have been privileged throughout my life to have wonderful mentors and research experiences and I am now committed to providing a safe, supportive environment for interns to explore the scientific world. Interns from all backgrounds are welcome to contact me about research opportunities! Remote opportunties are available.


Topics I have mentored interns on in the past include:

  • Experimental design
  • Microbial culturing and identification
  • Molecular biology techniques
  • Field work and field safety
  • Literature review and synthesis
  • Bioinformatic analyses via web-based, GUI and command-line tools
  • Documenting and troubleshooting bioinformatic pipelines
  • Oral and written communication of scientific results
  • Graduate school and fellowship applications
  • Mental health awareness and prioritization
  • Professional and academic networking
  • Personal values and skills inventory
  • Career development in STEM fields

Preprints and open science

I am also an advocate for the use of preprints, open science, and reproducibility. I serve as a Champion of the National Microbiome Data Collaborative where I promote Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable (FAIR) data principles in microbiome research. I was a 2021 ASAPbio Fellow during which time we wrote a "A Guide to Preprinting for Early Career Researchers" to help empower researchers to engage with preprints.

Science Communication

I am passionate about scientific communication at all levels. I was interviewed about marine fungi for the Mushroom Revival Podcast. I led an article for Frontiers for Young Minds, "Even Superheroes Need Help Sometimes: Three Incredible Tales of Microbial Symbiosis", which discusses different types of symbiosis using three microbe-animal systems. I was also involved in the development of a microbiome board game, Gut Check.

Expanding Your Horizons (EYH)

EYH conferences introduce 5th-8th grade girls to STEM careers. Since 2014, I have co-led the "Genome Detectives" workshop at UC Berkeley.
